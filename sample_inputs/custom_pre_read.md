To be honest, I’m really not sure how to make a call on this one, but the team is pushing hard to scale AI Agents across multiple operations teams fast. The pilots we ran on shift reports and maintenance troubleshooting actually seemed to cut down a lot of manual work. Some people are saying it saves a massive amount of time, but then again, we don’t have any clear baselines—like, how many hours were spent before, and exactly how much did the agents reduce it?
Operations is in a hurry because the frontline staff is complaining that finding SOPs and old incident records is a nightmare. But on the other hand, Risk and Compliance are feeling pretty uneasy; they’re worried about who’s actually accountable if an agent gives the wrong advice. I mean, searching for knowledge seems safe enough, but some use cases have agents starting to suggest actions or recommend next steps, and I can’t tell if that’s still just 'decision support' or if we're moving into 'autonomy' territory.
And then there's the data access—it's a mess. Some teams want the agent to read everything: maintenance logs, production data, SOPs, RCAs, and email summaries all at once, but nobody has clearly defined what's confidential, what’s okay to use, or what needs special approval first. The Digital team says the platform is ready-ish, but we don't have a full governance framework yet. HR is saying that for people to actually adopt this, training has to happen alongside real use cases, or they’ll just forget what they learned. But waiting for full governance might take too long, especially since the business wants to see productivity impact this year."
The CFO is also asking how we’re going to measure the benefits, because right now it’s just the word 'improvement' without any auditable numbers. Some people suggested scaling just the low-risk stuff first, like retrieval and summaries, but others argue the value there isn’t enough to justify the budget. One of the plant teams wants to try an agent for troubleshooting abnormal conditions, but I’m terrified that if the context is incomplete, the agent might answer with too much confidence, and that’s a huge risk. We don’t have clear 'halt rules' yet—like, when should the agent stop and ask an engineer or a supervisor?
The approval gates are still confusing too—who approves new agents? Who monitors the output? Who’s in charge of updating the knowledge base when an SOP changes? And if the agent uses outdated info, who takes the hit?
Bottom line: I feel the value is real, but the governance gap is huge. For the next Management Committee meeting, we need direction. Do we approve the scale-up? Do a targeted pilot? Or stop and wait for governance? If we stop, we lose momentum and everyone goes back to manual work. I need help summarizing this for the execs—not too long, but they need to see the trade-offs between speed and control, or maybe a middle ground that isn't just us lying to ourselves. And if we’re missing info, we need to say exactly what's missing before we decide for real.
